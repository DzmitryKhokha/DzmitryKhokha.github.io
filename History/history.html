BBQ: history

In a broad sense, the internationally recognized term barbecue combines all sorts of ways cooking products (traditionally meat, but the term has also spread to the preparation of fish, poultry, vegetables, fruits, desserts, toasting slices of bread) in the heat of coal, gas or electric heat heater - this is shish kebab, and grill, and kebab, and khorovats, and mtsvadi, and braai, and shurasko, and yakiniku. The word acquired this meaning in the 1940s and 50s in connection with the widespread suburban culture, primarily in English-speaking countries.

Voodoo and Haitian Roots

In fact, researchers are inclined to believe that the island of Haiti was the birthplace of barbecue. The green branches of tropical trees were used as fuel, which is why the meat acquired deep smoky taste. Spirits love the smell of meat

The ancient Haitian way was not only an opportunity to get delicious and fragrant meat, but also served several other purposes at once. Firstly, the aroma of slow-cooked meat was very loved by numerous spirits that were found in abundance in the islands of the Caribbean. Secondly, the smell of meat scared away wild animals, which in those days were no less than spirits. Thus, the barbecue combo effect played on the hand of its popularization and the method quickly became the property of European lovers of delicious. BBQ reservations

In the description of the indigenous peoples of North America, there are many references to the fact that the local Indians they are very fond of dancing, as well as "going to the forest to cook and eat barbecue there." First American President George Washington was very fond of eating meat on the coals and even organized mass barbecue meetings during his campaign period. When he won over the British and the independence of the United States was proclaimed, barbecues were held all over the country to celebrate. When Washington laid the stone in 1793 at the construction site of the Capitol, not only was a solemn parade, but also a whole bull weighing more than two hundred kilograms is roasted on a spit over the coals.

Cowboy food

The term "barbecue" began to gain popularity in the English language at the beginning of the 19th century, when the country began covered with a network of roads and numerous eateries appeared near them. Cook juicy steaks on charcoal was a common thing for cowboys - professional cattle drivers who ate the same as distilled.

Coal coup

A modern barbecue is, of course, a special hearth where you can cook both meat and any other products. An important moment in the history of the development of barbecue was the invention of a semi-finished wood product. coal, which played a very important role in the development of the culinary method. This charcoal has been patented in 1897 and was produced even at the factories of Henry Ford.

Spherical barbecue

In the 1950s, a special spherical barbecue cauldron was invented, which was invented by a simple welder from Chicago George Stephen. It was his prototype of the brazier that became the reference for barbecue and is used with small changes to this day. The shape of the brazier has become very successful for gas barbecue-themed modifications, which have also become very popular nowadays.
