Secrets of Using Grilling Spices

Among other things, the taste of a dish determines which spices and herbs are used in it. When it comes to grilling, their influence cannot be written off, and on the contrary, they should be selected more carefully. Spice therapist Lior Lev Serkazh, and at the same time a big fan of grilled dishes, knows this better than anyone else. While the fire season is still in full swing, the spice master dares to share his grilling secrets and signature seasoning recipes that are the perfect addition to any barbecue.

Use hotter spices

There is no better time to experiment with seasonings than summer. This is the time for bold, spicy and pungent scents. A universal seasoning with which it is impossible to miscalculate is onion and garlic. If you add them raw to salads or grilled vegetables, you will definitely not be mistaken in your choice. One of my favorite vegetable spices is cumin. It makes their aroma "meaty" and also perfectly complements grilled dishes.

Make your own spice mix

The grill loves different spice mixes, and this is a great reason to make a versatile mix from your favorite additions to dishes that is equally well suited for grilling meats, vegetables and fish, as well as salads. There are three basic mixing rules you need to remember before you rush into the spice mill for all your favorite jars. First, each spice you want to add must be tasted separately. Second, it is important to strike a balance; there should be no more than 3-5 spices that have sour, spicy and sweet notes, as well as a different texture. And thirdly, you should not grind all the spices into powder, some of them open up much better in the form of "coarse crumbs".

Throw a tortilla on the grill, your guests will love it

Whenever I grill for my friends and family, I roast something over the fire whose aroma literally drives everyone crazy - pita bread. I grease it with oil and season it before I put it on the grill. It can be eaten without everything, or used as a base for grilled fish and meat. By the end of the meal, the lavash will absorb all the juices from the meat and it will simply be impossible to tear it away from it.

Experiment with different grilling methods

For those who are not accustomed to cooking over an open fire, the grill instills fear. Grilling is very easy, though. And this can be done not only on the grate. Sometimes I cook directly over charcoal in a frying pan or in a saucepan, but this is just one of many ways. On the grill, you can smoke or, for example, bury onions and potatoes directly in the coals for an incredible smoky flavor.
